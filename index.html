Research agenda

Technology, attention, and responsible analytics in markets

My work asks how new technologies change what consumers search for, which alternatives they compare, and how markets become organized around emerging categories. I use digital trace data, Bayesian models, and quantitative marketing methods to study market transformation before sales fully respond.

Market formation

I study how disruptive product entry reorganizes category-level and product-level attention, especially in emerging technology markets such as electric vehicles.

Marketing analytics

I develop and apply Bayesian and machine-learning-based tools for search, advertising, sales, forecasting, and diffusion analysis.

AI platform governance

I examine how AI content moderation and platform policy affect engagement, retention, subscription demand, trust, and platform value.

Job market paper

Capturing the Impact of Tesla

Dynamic Factor Analysis Using Online Search, Advertising and Sales

Zhengwei (Harrison) Zhang, Jason A. Duan, and Garrett P. Sonnier

This paper examines how the introduction of the Tesla Model 3 reshaped consumer attention in the U.S. automobile market. Using weekly vehicle-level data from 2014 to 2021 that combine Google Trends search indices, Vivvix advertising expenditures, and Ward’s vehicle sales, I develop a Bayesian dynamic latent-factor model of search spillovers across the EV topic, Tesla Model 3, non-Tesla electric vehicles, and incumbent compact competitors.

The results show that the Model 3’s clearest empirical footprint appears in the search system rather than in immediate sales or advertising responses. After Model 3 entry, EV-topic attention became more persistent, Tesla Model 3 search became a major driver of broader EV interest, and the competitive search environment became more organized around EV-centered comparison paths.

Manuscript available upon request.
