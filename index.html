Job market paper

Capturing the Impact of Tesla: Dynamic Factor Analysis Using Online Search, Advertising and Sales

Zhengwei (Harrison) Zhang, Jason A. Duan, and Garrett P. Sonnier

This paper studies how the introduction of the Tesla Model 3 reshaped consumer attention in the U.S. automobile market. Using a weekly vehicle-level dataset from 2014 to 2021 that combines Google Trends search indices, Vivvix advertising expenditures, and Ward's vehicle sales, I develop a Bayesian dynamic factor model of latent consumer information states.

The results suggest that the Model 3's primary impact appears in the search system rather than in immediate sales or advertising responses. After Model 3 entry, EV-topic attention became more persistent, Model 3 search became a major driver of broader EV interest, and the competitive search environment became more organized around EV-centered comparison paths.
